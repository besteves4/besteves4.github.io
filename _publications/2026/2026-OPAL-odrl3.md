abstract: >-
  Growing use of machine-readable policy systems for digital content and data governance highlights the need for robust, interoperable policy languages, yet <a href="https://www.w3.org/TR/odrl-model/" target="_blank">ODRL</a> 2.2 is limited by ambiguous concepts, a lack of formal semantics, and challenges in automated policy evaluation. Analysis of a music distribution use case identifies key conceptual and operational shortcomings and argues that future versions should adopt formal semantics, simplified abstractions, and standardized evaluation mechanisms to enable consistent, scalable, and interoperable policy evaluation.
